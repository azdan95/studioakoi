# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective clients, collaborators, and studios evaluating Ahmed Shahdan / AKOI Studio for interior architecture work — residential, hospitality, and conceptual spatial design, primarily in and around Malé, Maldives. They arrive on desktop or mobile, often from a referral, an Instagram link, or a direct share, with one question: *is this someone whose taste and craft I trust with a space?* The job to be done is a fast, confident read on point-of-view and competence — enough to reach out. Secondary audience: peers and recruiters scanning experience and tool fluency (Revit, Rhino/Grasshopper, V-Ray, the full stack).

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that earns a meeting. It exists to convert a glance into trust: present the work (spa pavilion, event pavilion, hotel lobby, cultural center, forest retreat), the person behind it, the services offered, and a low-friction way to make contact. Success is a qualified inquiry — someone who has felt the quality of the work and the deliberateness of the design, and writes to azdan95@gmail.com. The site itself is a portfolio piece: how it's made is evidence of how its author thinks about space, proportion, and detail.

## Brand Personality

**Refined · calm · precise.** Architect-as-craftsman: quiet authority over volume. The voice is composed and exact — no hype, no superlatives, no "award-winning solutions." Confidence is shown through restraint and the quality of every edge, not asserted in copy. The emotional goal is *settled trust* — the visitor should feel they're in steady, discerning hands.

The signature elements (the ember-orange `#EA5C1F`, the living lava-lamp hero backdrop with its wave-physics flow field, the custom cursor) are deliberate identity, not decoration — they make the surface feel alive and authored. But they are used with control: loud where it signs the work, quiet everywhere the work itself should speak. Warmth carries through the ember accent and the cream/ember washes, never through clutter.

## Anti-references

- **The minimalist all-white gallery.** A sterile grid of images on white with no personality, indistinguishable from a thousand other portfolios. This site must feel *authored and warm*, not clinical-empty. (Primary anti-reference — confirmed by the user.)
- **Generic Squarespace/Wix template portfolios** — centered hero, uniform card grid, stock everything, the visible default.
- **Corporate architecture-firm gravitas** — navy/gray palettes, stock-photo solemnity, committee copy.
- **Overdesigned agency showreels** — motion for motion's sake, slow, unreadable, all flash and no substance. (The motion here must always serve calm and craft, never spectacle.)

## Design Principles

1. **The surface is a portfolio piece.** Every detail — edges, proportion, timing, type — is evidence of how its author treats a space. Hold it to the standard of the work it presents.
2. **Quiet authority over volume.** Confidence is demonstrated through restraint and precision, not asserted with size or hype. Let the work and the whitespace do the talking.
3. **Warmth, not sterility.** Reject the clinical white gallery. The ember accent, warm washes, and the living backdrop keep the surface human and authored — controlled warmth, never clutter.
4. **Motion as signature, used with control.** The animated hero and cursor are core identity and stay. Loud where they sign the work; quiet everywhere the work itself should lead. Motion always serves calm and craft.
5. **One read to trust.** Optimize for the fast, confident judgment a prospective client makes — clear point-of-view, visible competence, frictionless contact.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA** for contrast (≥4.5:1 body, ≥3:1 large text), keyboard navigation, and focus states. Note the custom cursor hides the native cursor (`cursor: none`) — ensure keyboard and focus affordances remain fully usable for anyone not driving with a mouse.

Motion is core identity, so the heavy hero animation stays as the default rather than being diluted. Provide a genuine `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` fallback (the living backdrop settles to a static warm wash; transitions become crossfades or instant) so reduced-motion users get a calm, complete page — never a blank or broken one.
